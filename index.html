Is Sugar Consumption Linked to Diabetes?

This is a collection of data of the year 2017 of people between the age 20 to 79.

There is no clear cause of diabetes. However, too much sugar can lead to overweight and high blood sugar which are risk factors for developing diabetes.

It's not a myth!

Eating the right foods can lead to a longer, healthier life. To increase your lifespan, check out the major food groups that help you live longer. With the right contribution of food you can aim for a healthy balanced diet.

40%
Breads, pasta and cereals
Nutrient(s): Carbohydrates
At least 3 servings a day which the body uses for energy. Good choices are wholemeal, wholegrain bread, brown rice, couscous and polenta.
35%
Fruit and vegetables
Nutrient(s): Vitamins, minerals and fibre
At least 3 servings of vegetables and 2 servings of fruits of different colours to provide a range of vitamins and minerals. One daily portion can be replaced with 2dl fruit juice with no additional sugar.
20%
Meat, fish, milk and eggs
Nutrient(s): Protein, calcium, iron and zinc
2 to 3 servings a day with 1 serving of meat and at least 1 serving of Milk or alternatives. Having enough calcium is necessary for healthy bones and teeth.
5%
Fat, oil, sugar, sweets
Nutrient(s): Fat, sugar and/or salt
In very small amounts because they have only a little nutritional value. They should be consumed in moderation.

0%
Alcohol and other
Nutrient(s): Fat, sugar and/or salt
In very small amounts because they have only a little nutritional value. They should be consumed in moderation.
